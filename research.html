Research

My research focuses on machine learning and optimization, with an emphasis on survival modeling, counterfactual analysis, and actionable AI for decision support in healthcare, insurance, workforce planning, and financial services.

Current Research Projects

Predictive Analytics in Actuarial Science

Customer lapse, or the cancellation of life insurance policies, is a critical challenge for insurers, impacting their profitability and long-term sustainability. This project uses machine learning models to predict the likelihood of customer lapses, utilizing data such as policy characteristics, customer demographics, and historical interactions. Our approach employs XGBoost for enhanced predictive accuracy, complemented by feature engineering techniques to capture relevant factors. Additionally, we apply SHAP (Shapley Additive Explanations) to provide explainability in model predictions, enabling stakeholders to understand the key drivers of lapse risk. By offering actionable insights, this model helps insurers reduce churn, optimize customer retention strategies, and ultimately improve profitability.

Conterfactual Optimization for Bone Marrow Transplant Matching

Diseases requiring bone marrow transplants (BMTs), also called stem cell transplants, include leukemia, lymphoma, sickle cell disease and aplastic anemia, as well as some immunodeficiencies. The success of a bone marrow transplant is uncertain, and depends on many factors, including underlying diagnosis, health status, donor relation, etc. Other important factors may exist that are not yet known or well-understood by clinicians. By examining historical records of BMTs, we develop machine learning tools to predict the success of a BMT with a particular patient and donor, using only data regularly collected during the course of treatment. We transform these predictions into conventional Kaplan-Meier survival functions to help clinicians and patients understand individualized survival probabilities and select the best course of treatment.

Evaluating Survival Models Beyond the C-Index

Developing robust evaluation frameworks for survival analysis, with a focus on the limitations of the Concordance Index (C-index) and the introduction of new metrics such as Area Under the Survival Curve (AUSC). This work aims to align model evaluation with individualized risk estimation and time-to-event prediction goals.

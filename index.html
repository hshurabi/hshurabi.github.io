Biography

Hamed Shourabizadeh is a researcher in applied machine learning and operations research, specializing in data-driven decision-making for complex and high-stakes environments. He received his Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering from the University of Toronto, where he developed machine learning frameworks to improve individualized outcome prediction and intervention planning in clinical and actuarial settings. He also holds an M.Sc. from Sharif University of Technology (2015) and a B.Sc. from Iran University of Science and Technology (2013). Prior to his doctoral studies, he worked on a TÜBİTAK research project at Özyeğin University, where he remains closely engaged with the academic community.

Dr. Shourabizadeh's research spans healthcare operations, insurance analytics, workforce planning, and survival modeling, with particular emphasis on turning predictive models into actionable recommendations. His work combines techniques from machine learning, optimization, and statistical modeling to support better decision-making across sectors. He is especially interested in counterfactual intervention modeling, explainable AI for temporal data, and individualized survival analysis. He has presented his work at international conferences including CORS/INFORMS and EBMT, and has held applied data science roles in both academic and industry settings.
